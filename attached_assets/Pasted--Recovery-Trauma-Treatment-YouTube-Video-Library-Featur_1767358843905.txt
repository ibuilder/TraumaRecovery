### Finding Specific Content

- **For treatment program videos**: Search program name + “tour” or “testimonials”
- **For clinical education**: Search clinician name + “lecture” or “presentation”
- **For personal stories**: Search “recovery story” + specific addiction/trauma type
- **For therapy explanations**: Search therapy modality + “explained” (e.g., “EMDR explained”)

## Tips for Using This Library

1. **Subscribe & Create Playlists** - Subscribe to channels that resonate with your recovery journey and create topic-specific playlists (trauma, addiction types, specific modalities)
1. **Take Notes & Journal** - Keep a recovery journal noting insights that are meaningful, quotes that resonate, and practices you want to try
1. **Pace Yourself** - Trauma content can be activating and re-traumatizing. Watch in safe environments, have support available, and take breaks when needed
1. **Share Mindfully** - Share helpful videos with others in recovery, but be mindful of where people are in their journey
1. **Verify Current Information** - Treatment approaches evolve. Cross-reference older videos with current best practices and research
1. **Check Video Dates** - Some content may be older. Look for more recent uploads for current research and approaches
1. **Combine with Professional Help** - Videos are educational but not substitutes for therapy. Use them to supplement professional treatment
1. **Explore Related Content** - YouTube’s algorithm will suggest related videos. This can help you discover new clinicians and resources
1. **Use Transcripts & Captions** - Many videos have transcripts you can read instead of watching, which can be less triggering
1. **Join Online Communities** - Many of these programs have Facebook groups, Discord servers, or other online communities for additional support
